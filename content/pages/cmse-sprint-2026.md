## Overview

The first-ever CMSE Curriculum Development Sprint is a collaborative work week taking place **June 8--12, 2026** in **STEM 1201** (with Zoom available as needed). Building on insights from two rounds of focus group discussions, the sprint brings together CMSE instructors, researchers, and graduate students for structured, hands-on sessions aimed at meaningfully improving our curriculum.

Sessions run **10:00 am -- 4:00 pm** each day and are organized around three potential axes of development:

- **Classroom structure and pedagogical approaches** — how we teach and engage students
- **Course content and included topics** — what we teach and why
- **Technical implementation and accessibility** — how materials are built and delivered

This is a low-stakes, high-impact effort. You are encouraged to attend as much or as little as your schedule allows — even partial participation is valuable. If you cannot join sprint sessions in person, there will also be opportunities to join a sprint team and continue this work through the summer.

Select sessions will also include dedicated **professional development** opportunities focused on instructional design and pedagogical approaches, giving participants practical tools and frameworks they can bring directly back to their teaching.

Participation in the sprint is designed to support your professional work in concrete ways:

- **Documentable service** — a clear indicator of commitment to the department's teaching mission for annual evaluations
- **Portfolio building** — a mechanism to generate tangible materials (new modules, peer-reviewed pedagogy, or assessment tools) that can support reappointment, promotion, and tenure cases; graduate students can draw on this experience for future faculty job applications

Questions? Reach out to [Devin Silvia](mailto:dsilvia@msu.edu) or [Sona Chitchyan](mailto:chitchyan@msu.edu).
